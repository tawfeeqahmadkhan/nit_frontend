# Product

## Register

product

## Users

B2B business owners and operators onboarding their company onto SolveNet. Primary context: registering for the first time, filling in company details and tagging services/challenges. Secondary: reviewing profile data before submitting. Users are motivated, goal-oriented — they want setup done fast so AI can start finding matches. Not tech-averse, but not developers either.

## Product Purpose

SolveNet is a B2B AI matchmaking platform. It analyzes business services and challenges to surface high-quality cross-business connections. The registration form is the activation funnel: onboarding quality directly determines match quality. Success = user completes all 4 steps and submits with confidence.

## Brand Personality

Precise, efficient, credible. Orange-accented without shouting. Feels like a well-built professional tool, not a startup splash page.

## Anti-references

- Mixed color systems (green services + red challenges + orange CTAs + grey cards in the same view) — visual noise
- Glassmorphism, gradient overlays, decorative separator lines
- SaaS-cream + rainbow-tag patterns (Notion-style colorful tags)
- Rounded-everything card-within-card nesting
- Heavy drop shadows as decoration

## Design Principles

1. **One palette, consistent signal.** Orange is the only accent. Semantic color only for hard error states (invalid input). Everything else is neutral + orange.
2. **Labels carry meaning.** Service vs. challenge distinction lives in the section label, not the tag color. UI chrome stays neutral so content reads clearly.
3. **Elevation through restraint.** Cards distinguish surfaces with 1px borders and 8px shadows, not background-color variance. The page bg cream + white surfaces + subtle shadow = the full elevation system.
4. **Progress is visible.** The step indicator and progress bar must always communicate where the user is and how far they've come — no dead or ambiguous states.
5. **Touch-first sizing.** CTA buttons and interactive elements meet 44px touch targets. Form usable at 375px without horizontal scroll.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Focus states on all interactive elements. Error messages tied to form fields. No color-only distinction for semantic meaning (services vs. challenges relying solely on green/red — use labels).
